# A survey of Behavior Trees in robotics and AI

Behavior Trees (BTs) were originally invented to enable modular AI in computer games but have gained significant attention in the robotics community over the last decade. 

As agent AI complexity increases, traditional **Finite State Machines (FSMs)** often scale poorly and become difficult to extend or reuse. BTs address these issues by:
- Organizing state transition logic in a **hierarchical tree structure**.
- Organizing states as leaves, which significantly improves **modularity**.
- Simplifying both synthesis and analysis for humans and algorithms.

This paper provides a comprehensive survey of BTs in AI and Robotic applications, categorizing existing literature based on methods, application areas, and contributions, and identifying open research challenges.

## Key Comparison
- **FSM**: Logic is dispersed across individual states; difficult to scale and reuse.
- **BT**: Logic is hierarchical and centralized; highly modular and easy to analyze.

## Research Areas
- Methods for synthesis and analysis.
- Application areas in AI and Robotics.
- Learning Behavior Trees.
